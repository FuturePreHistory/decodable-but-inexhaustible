# Decodable but Inexhaustible

[![DOI](https://zenodo.org/badge/DOI/10.5281/zenodo.20995166.svg)](https://doi.org/10.5281/zenodo.20995166)


可解码而不可穷尽：一种未来智能测试及《红楼梦》案例

This repository archives the initial canonical draft of “Decodable but Inexhaustible,” a theoretical essay on external civilization decodability, future intelligence testing, and the classical work as a decodable yet inexhaustible civilizational information structure.


## Core claim

A strong classical work can remain decodable by future readers, external intelligence systems, or unfamiliar interpretive systems after it has been detached from its original historical context. At the same time, it can preserve forms of experience, affect, symbolic residue, and aesthetic density that cannot be exhausted by information extraction, conceptual modeling, or civilizational reconstruction.


## Central concept

External civilization decodability refers to the capacity of a work to allow future readers, external intelligence systems, or other interpretive systems to reconstruct, infer, contest, and reactivate traces of civilizational structure after the work has been separated from its original audience, historical conditions, and interpretive community.

## Case study

The essay uses Dream of the Red Chamber as a high-density test case. It examines how the novel exposes kinship order, spatial hierarchy, gender discipline, resource circulation, ritual structure, poetic practice, decline consciousness, and irreducible affective residue.
